Introduction

The Defence Budget of India is a crucial aspect of national security and military modernization. It determines the country's capability to maintain and enhance its armed forces. Every year, a significant portion of the national budget is allocated towards defence, ensuring the security of the nation against external and internal threats. The budget includes allocations for the Army, Navy, Air Force, Defence Research and Development, and other related expenses. It plays a vital role in strengthening India's defence infrastructure, acquiring new technologies, and ensuring the welfare of personnel. Additionally, defence spending influences economic factors such as job creation, technological advancements, and international collaborations in the defence sector.
