Department of Economic Affairs





Key Responsibilities of the Department:

  • Formulation of Economic Policies:
    Develops and implements macroeconomic policies to ensure sustainable economic growth.
  • Budget Preparation & Fiscal Policy:
    Oversees the Union Budget, taxation policies, and fiscal management strategies.
  • Capital Market Regulation:
    Supervises stock exchanges, financial institutions, and investment policies.
  • External Financial Relations:
    Manages foreign exchange reserves, international trade policies, and financial agreements with global institutions (IMF, World Bank).


Changes to Economic Affairs in the 2025 Budget Session

  • Boost to Infrastructure Investment :
    A 20% increase in public infrastructure spending to accelerate economic growth and job creation.
  • Revised Taxation Policies:
    New tax reforms introduced to encourage foreign direct investment (FDI) and ease compliance for businesses.

Frequently Asked Questions

How will the 2025 budget impact economic growth?

The budget prioritizes infrastructure investment, tax reforms, and digital economy expansion, leading to increased job creation and economic stability.

What measures are being taken to control inflation?

The government has introduced revised monetary policies in collaboration with the RBI, focusing on interest rate adjustments and market liquidity management.

How does the budget support MSMEs and startups?

The budget provides financial incentives, low-interest loans, and simplified tax structures to promote the growth of small businesses and entrepreneurship.
